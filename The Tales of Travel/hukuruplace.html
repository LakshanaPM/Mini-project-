Tourist Places in Hukuru Miskiy

Things To Do

Intricate Coral Stone Architecture

About

The mosque is constructed using interlocking coral blocks, adorned with detailed Quranic inscriptions and geometric patterns. The walls are supported by cut coral columns, and the vaulted ceiling showcases the artistry of Maldivian carpenters.

Adjacent Blue-and-White Minaret

About

Built in 1675, the mosque's minaret is a distinctive feature with its blue-and-white color scheme and a design resembling a wedding cake. Made of coral stones and braced with metal strips, it stands as a testament to the architectural prowess of the era.

Surrounding Coral Tombstones

About

The mosque is encircled by a graveyard featuring intricately carved coral tombstones. The design of the tombstones varies: rounded tops signify female graves, pointed tops denote male graves, and gilded inscriptions indicate royal burials.

Medhu Ziyaaraiy

About

Located adjacent to the mosque, this tomb is dedicated to the Islamic scholar believed to have introduced Islam to the Maldives. The site is a place of pilgrimage and is often draped in green cloth, symbolizing respect in Islamic tradition

UNESCO Tentative World Heritage Status

About

Recognized for its unique architecture and cultural significance, Hukuru Miskiy was added to the UNESCO Tentative World Heritage list in 2008. The mosque exemplifies the creative excellence and achievement of the Maldivian people in architecture and artistry
