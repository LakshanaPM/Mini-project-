Things To Do

Eravikulam National Park , Munnar

About

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Eravikulam National Park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and scenic landscapes. The park is home to the endangered Nilgiri tahr (Nilgiritragus hylocrius), a mountain goat species endemic to the region . Visitors can embark on treks that offer panoramic views of rolling grasslands and shola forests. A notable feature is the blooming of Neelakurinji flowers, which paint the hillsides blue once every 12 years.

Edakkal Caves , Wayanad

About

Perched 1,200 meters above sea level, the Edakkal Caves are not traditional caves but rock shelters formed by a massive boulder wedged between two others. Inside, visitors can observe prehistoric petroglyphs dating back to at least 6,000 BCE, depicting human figures, animals, and symbols . The site offers a glimpse into ancient human settlements and is accessible via a short trek.

Lighthouse Beach , Kovalam

About

Kovalam's Lighthouse Beach is renowned for its crescent-shaped shoreline and the iconic red-and-white striped lighthouse. The beach is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying fresh seafood. The lighthouse offers panoramic views of the Arabian Sea, making it a popular spot for both relaxation and photography.

The Backwaters Cruise , Alleppey

About

Alleppey, often referred to as the "Venice of the East," offers tranquil backwater cruises through a network of canals, lagoons, and paddy fields. Traveling in a traditional houseboat provides an intimate experience of Kerala's rural life and natural beauty. The serene environment, combined with local cuisine onboard, makes for a memorable journey .
