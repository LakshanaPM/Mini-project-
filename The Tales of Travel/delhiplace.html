Tourist Places in New Delhi

Things To Do

Qutub Minar

About

Standing at 73 meters, the Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world. Constructed in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and later completed by Iltutmish, it exemplifies Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture. The minaret features intricate carvings and inscriptions from the Qur'an. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination, attracting millions annually.

ISKCON Temple

About

Located in East of Kailash, this temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha. Inaugurated in 1998, it serves as a spiritual and cultural hub, offering devotional services, cultural programs, and a Vedic museum. The temple's architecture is a blend of traditional and modern styles, providing a serene environment for devotees and visitors.

Delhi Zoo

About

Delhi Zoo (National Zoological Park): Established in 1959, the zoo houses a diverse range of animals and birds, including endangered species. It's a favorite spot for families and children to learn about wildlife conservation.

Red Fort

About

Built between 1638 and 1648 by Emperor Shah Jahan, the Red Fort is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Constructed from red sandstone, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. The fort complex includes various structures like the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience) and Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience). It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2007

India Gate

About

Standing at 42 meters, India Gate is a war memorial dedicated to the 70,000 Indian soldiers who died during World War I. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it features the names of over 13,000 soldiers inscribed on its surface. An eternal flame, Amar Jawan Jyoti, burns beneath the arch to honor the soldiers who died in the Indo-Pakistan War of 1971.
